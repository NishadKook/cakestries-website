The Studio

A small atelier with an unhurried hand.

Cakestries is a bespoke cake studio founded by Chantale Abbott-Keith in Toronto — where art, architecture, and pastry craft meet to create cakes that are as beautiful to look at as they are to taste.

I — Our Story

It started with a love for art, fashion, and architecture.

Chantale always had a creative eye — drawn to art, fashion, and the precision of architecture. When she discovered pastry arts, something clicked: here was a medium that let her combine every creative discipline into one craft.

What began as a passion quickly became a practice. She learned that the magic was never only in how a cake looked, but in the moment it was cut — when something beautiful turned out to taste like the best thing in the room.

Today, Cakestries serves the Greater Toronto Area and Peel Region, with over 17,000 followers on Instagram and commissions for every milestone — from intimate birthdays to grand weddings. Each one designed from scratch, finished by hand.

II What We Hold To

The principles behind every cake.

01

Designed, not duplicated

No catalogue, no copy-paste. Every commission is drawn for one occasion and retired after it.

02

Flavour first

Beauty is the invitation; taste is the promise. We develop both in the same breath, from scratch.

03

Made by hand

Sugar petals, gilding, sculpted form — finished by one pair of hands, the slow way, every time.

04

Honest ingredients

Real butter, real fruit, seasonal where we can. Nothing on the plate we wouldn't serve our own table.

05

Few, done fully

We cap our week on purpose. Fewer cakes, more attention — the only way to keep the standard.

06

Your story, centred

The brief is always you — your palette, your people, the feeling you want left in the room.

The maker
III — The Hand Behind It

From architecture
to cake artistry.

Chantale trained in pastry arts after years immersed in art, fashion, and architecture. That cross-disciplinary eye is the studio's signature — cakes built with an architect's precision and finished with a fashion designer's sense of detail.

Beyond the studio, Chantale is the creator of Cake & Worship — a community experience that brings together cake decorating, Christian worship, and connection. She has spoken on panels celebrating Black-owned artisan businesses at PAMA, and continues to teach workshops across the GTA.
